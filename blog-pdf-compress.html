How to Safely Compress PDF Files for Email in 2026

By Eebii Team | February 2026 | Category: Pdf

We've all been there: you compose the perfect professional email to a client, attach your pitch deck or finalized contract, hit send—and get immediately bounced back with an error stating "Attachment exceeds maximum size allowed."

Major email providers like Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo typically restrict attachments to 20MB or 25MB. But business PDFs loaded with high-resolution graphics and scans easily blow past that limit.

The Risk of Unsafe Compression

Your first instinct is likely to Google "compress PDF online." This is where many businesses unknowingly risk severe data breaches.

  • Loss of Confidentiality: Most "free" tools upload your files to their foreign remote server to process the compression. You have no idea who is reading your confidential financial digits or HR policies.
  • Unusable Quality: Some automated tools drastically reduce image quality, turning professional logos and high-res schematics into blurry, pixelated messes.
  • Watermarks: You download the compressed version only to discover a massive third-party logo plastered on every page.
